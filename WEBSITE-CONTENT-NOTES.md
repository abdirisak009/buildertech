### 2.1 Hero
- **Cinwaan:** Engineers who draw. Architects who build.

### 2.2 Sheekada shirkadda (Our story)
> Builders Tech opened in 2012 as a three-person architectural studio working on private villas. The turning point came on our fourth commercial commission, when a coordination error between the structural and mechanical consultants cost the client four months.
>
> We decided never to sit on the wrong side of that problem again. We hired structural and MEP engineers in-house, moved every project into a single federated BIM model, and rebuilt our workflow so that no drawing is issued before it has been checked against every other discipline.
>
> Fourteen years later we are a multidisciplinary practice of 40+ professionals delivering commercial, residential, healthcare and public projects — and coordination is still the thing we are known for.

### 2.3 Himilada iyo Hadafka (Mission & Vision)
- **Mission:**
  > To remove uncertainty from building — by designing, engineering and supervising under one accountable roof, so our clients know the cost, the programme and the outcome before the first excavation.

## 5. BOGGA PROCESS (Habraaca)

Ujeeddo: macmiilku ha ogaado waxa dhici doona tallaabo tallaabo — waqti, deliverable, iyo cida wax go'aamisa.

### 5.1 Hero
- **Cinwaan:** Five stages. A signed gate at the end of each one.
- **Faqrad:**
  > Every Builders Tech project runs on the same method. At the end of each stage you receive a written deliverable and you sign it off before we spend your money on the next one.

### 5.2 Tallaabooyinka (5)

**Stage 1 — Discovery & Brief** · *2–4 weeks*
> We measure the site, test the soil, check what the regulations allow and write down what you actually need. Nothing is designed until the brief is agreed in writing.
- Site survey and measured drawings
- Geotechnical investigation
- Regulatory and zoning review
- Written project brief and success criteria
- **Gate:** client signs the brief

**Stage 2 — Concept Design** · *3–6 weeks*
> Two or three genuinely different options, presented with the trade-offs made explicit — cost, buildability, programme.
- Massing and layout options
- 3D visualisation and walkthrough
- Order-of-magnitude cost estimate
- Preliminary programme
- **Gate:** client selects the concept

**Stage 3 — Technical Design** · *8–16 weeks*
> Everything is modelled, coordinated and clash-checked. This is where the project is really won or lost.
- Full BIM coordination across all disciplines
- Structural and MEP design and calculations
- Permit submission and authority liaison
- Tender-ready drawings and specifications
- Quantity take-off and cost plan
- **Gate:** permit granted, tender documents issued

**Stage 4 — Construction** · *depends on scope*
> We tender the work, help you pick the contractor, then stay on site and check that what is built matches what was approved.
- Tender analysis and contractor recommendation
- Weekly site inspection and quality control
- Material approvals and testing
- Certified progress payments
- Variation and change control
- **Gate:** practical completion certificate

**Stage 5 — Handover & Support** · *4–8 weeks + 12 months*
> The building is only finished when you can operate it without us.
- Snagging and defect close-out
- Systems commissioning and staff training
- As-built drawings and digital twin
- O&M manuals and warranty register
- 12-month defects liability support
- **Gate:** final handover file accepted

### 5.3 Su'aalaha badanaa la weydiiyo (FAQ)
**How long does a typical project take?**
> A private villa runs 12–16 months from brief to handover. A mid-rise commercial building is typically 24–30 months. The design stages are 3–6 months of that; the rest is construction.

**What does the design fee usually cost?**
> Design and engineering fees generally land between 5% and 12% of construction cost, depending on complexity and how much supervision you want. We quote a fixed fee per stage after Discovery, so you are never billed for an open-ended scope.

**Can you take over a project that has already started?**
> Yes. We audit what exists — drawings, model, cost plan and site condition — and issue a written assessment before agreeing to take responsibility for it.

**Do we have to use your contractor?**
> No. We do not own a construction company and we take no commission from contractors. We evaluate tenders and give you a recommendation; the appointment is yours.

**What happens if the budget does not work?**
> You hear it in Discovery, not in month nine. If the brief and the budget do not reconcile, we tell you what has to change — scope, quality or programme — before design begins.

**Who owns the drawings and the model?**
> You do, on final payment. You receive the native model files, not only PDFs.
